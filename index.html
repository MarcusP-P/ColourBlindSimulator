Introduction

This site, by its nature will be inaccessible, as it requres vision.

People who are colourblind see things differently to people who don't suffer colour vision deficiencies. While there are several sites that simulate colourblindness, none of them seem to represent what I see. This is an attempt to create a version that represents what I see.

Flatla & Gutwin technique

Flatla and Gutwin proposed a technique that requires a calibration to model the colourblindness, and then use that to process images to simulate the colourblindness (2012). The calibration technique displays a circle with 1/8 missing, and the user selects the section that is missing (Flatla & Gutwin, 2011). Their base patern is a grey, and the other colours are selected using binary search along the lines of confusion and then estimating an elipse, that contains the colours that a person who is colour vision deficient can't difrentiate.

In this initial version, instead of testing along the lines of confusion, I will be using the appraoch of radiating out from the background colour in 12 directions, which was determiend by Birch, Barbur & Harlow to be adequate for most purposes(, 2007). The rest of the calibration will follow Flatla & Gutwin's approach.
